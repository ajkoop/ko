Spring 2015 Syllabus

Professional Expectations

IU Journalism is accredited by the Accrediting Council on Education in Journalism and Mass Communications (ACEJMC), which reviews its program every six years. Receiving accredited status is an indication that the Schoolâ€™s courses for journalism majors are addressing the values and competencies established by the ACEJMC.

The list of twelve values and competencies can generally be applied in total to J360, however the following five are particularly important to achieve for the course:

  • Understand concepts and apply theories in the use and presentation of images and information;
  • Think critically, creatively and independently;
  • Write correctly and clearly in forms and styles appropriate for the communications professions, audiences and purposes they serve;
  • Critically evaluate their own work and that of others for accuracy and fairness, clarity, appropriate style and grammatical correctness;
  • Apply tools and technologies appropriate for the communications professions in which they work.

Goalsetting

Some great portfolios convey dominance in a particular area and others showcase the power of fusing talents together. In our first week, I would like you to think about your academic and professional career up to this very moment, assess where you are, and where you want to go. How will your portfolio get you there? Who is your target audience and what do you want to convey?

Important Dates

Assignment deadlines and quiz dates are listed below. They are all due on the date listed at 11:59pm. Make certain to read each assignment carefully to see whether or not classmate feedback is required. Please understand that I do not accept late assignments. If you fail to submit by the deadline, you will not receive credit for the assignment. Feedback assignments are an especially critical element in this course. The strict policy on assignment deadlines aims to ensure that each student receives equal feedback and an excellent chance of producing a quality portfolio. Missed assignments not only hurt your grade, but also your classmates’ ability to develop a portfolio website.

March 10: Syllabus Quiz

Read through the syllabus. A multiple choice quiz is due March 10th.

March 11: Learning Contract

By midnight, submit a 300 word learning contract in Canvas (learning management system).

In your contract, please discuss your goals for this course. Please also briefly detail your previous history with code. Feel free to include links to some of your favorite websites.

This week, begin to think about the pieces of work that you would like to include in your portfolio. This is an important step, because it shapes the look and feel of your eventual website.

March 13: Learning Contract Feedback

By end of day, please post thoughtful feedback to at least two of your classmates. Feedback is essential when building any type of skill. Be as honest and encouraging as possible. For full credit, you should suggest edits, alternative styles, or post links to helpful tutorials.

Spring Break: March 15-22

March 23: Research and Forum Posting

Visit the Wordpress Theme Repository and choose a few themes that you like. Write a 300-word post telling your classmates which theme or template that you have chosen and why.

March 25: Theme Research Feedback

By end of day, please post thoughtful feedback to at least two of your classmates. Feedback is essential when building any type of skill. Be as honest and encouraging as possible. For full credit, you should suggest edits, alternative styles, or post links to helpful tutorials.

March 27: Wordpress 101 (10 points)

Read through the assigned Wordpress 101 materials carefully. A multiple choice quiz is due March 27th.

April 1: Site Configuration and Media Upload

This assignment begins the process of uploading portfolio-ready work and the configuration of specific site settings detailed in the course LMS (Canvas). By end of day, you must write a reflection post (300 words minimum) that discusses your plans to activate plugins and upload media.

April 3: Wordpress Skill Assessment

Please watch the Wordpress tutorials available at http://mediaschool.indiana.edu/learn. These tutorials will help you answer quiz questions related to the following:

  • category creation
  • post creation and management
  • page creation and management
  • widgets
  • menus
  • site options
  • plugins

April 8: Wordpress Questions and Confirmations

This is a time and place to post your Wordpress questions that might be difficult to article in an email or in a chat. Please post (300 words minimum) to tell the class what you have learned thus far and what you continue to struggle with.

I do not require feedback posts on this assignment, but I hope that you will write back to a number of your classmates to provide encouragement and suggestion.

April 13: Chat and Tutorial Week Begins

This week focuses on the remaining questions that you have for your instructor and classmates.

April 20: First Draft

The first draft of your website is due at midnight. Post a link to your site.

Along with your link, you should write a 300-word post that describes the customizations that you have made to your site. Rather than a bullet list of changes, focus on your major changes and explain the rationale behind your decisions.

Feel free to ask specific or general questions of your instructor or your classmates as you prepare to turn in your final website project on May 4th.

April 23: First Draft Feedback

Comment on each of your classmates’ projects. Look at each site with the eye of a proofreader and an artist. This is your last chance to receive feedback from your classmates, so please do you your best to provide sincere, thoughtful feedback.

May 4: Final Portfolio Website Due

In these final weeks, you will work to respond to your classmates’ and instructor’s feedback by refining your website. Post a link to your final project to Canvas. Along with your link, please post a 300 word concluding statement describing what you have learned in the course. Have your goals changed as a result of your learning process?
